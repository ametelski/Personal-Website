About Me

United States Navy
Honor • Courage • Commitment

In January 2003 I joined the Navy and my first was station on the USS Abraham Lincoln in Everett Washington. I was initially an Airman (“a green shirt”), working on the flight deck launching and recovering aircraft. As this position was dangers it really enforced the importance of safety in my mind as there was little to no margin of error. After two years in the role, I deiced to a pursue a path as an Engineman Maintenance Technician. For the next two years, I was responsible for the operation and maintenance of four emergency diesel generators that could power the entire ship if needed.

The Navy provided me many opportunities and experiences that has shaped me into the person that I am now, and I am very grateful that could serve my country.

Audio Visual

After leaving the Navy in 2007, I started a career in the Audio Video (AV) industry. Applying the discipline I learned in the Navy and my drive to learn, I quickly progressed from a junior technician to a Project Manager. With 10 years of intensive audio visual, I gained vast knowledge and experience in the integration of high end corporate boardrooms, auditoriums, teleconferencing, and hospitality assemblies. My expertise grew in custom integrations, audio DSP’s, field terminations, rack fabrication, and commissioning of AV systems. While working in the AV industry I was exposed to many custom software for AV equipment that required fundamental knowledge of computer logic. This interaction coupled with working with people in the software/IT industry fueled my interest in becoming a software developer.
